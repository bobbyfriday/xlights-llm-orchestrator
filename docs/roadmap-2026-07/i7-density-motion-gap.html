1. Why

The 2026-06-11 corpus study (docs/effects-layering-analysis.md — 130,229 effects mined from 17 community/vendor .xsq files vs 2,265 effects across our two generated shows) found the generated fabric inverted relative to community practice: community shows are ~58% continuous-motion effects at ~1,300 effects/min placed as 0.3–0.9s beat-quantized cells; ours were ~16% motion at ~270 effects/min, dominated by punctuation (On 22%, Twinkle 23%) over long washes. That analysis drove a burst of changes (cell fabric, counterphase chases, motion value curves, blend modes, transitions, duration taxonomy v0.3) which partially closed the gap — but nobody has re-measured since. The number that justified the whole cell-fabric paradigm is a month stale, the tuning dials in weave.py/tuning.py were set by judgment rather than against a measurement, and improve-musicality Phase 2 is about to make “density” a per-treatment concept. This item is: re-measure, then tune against the measurement — with treatment-aware targets, not one global number.

2.4 A fresh data point: the golden fixture, measured today

The corpus-comparison metrics can be computed from any instruction list. Running them over tests/fixtures/golden_instructions.json (the hermetic 24s, 2-section fixture) gives:

total: 103 instructions over 24s  ≈ 258 effects/min
by type: On 40, Twinkle 24, SingleStrand 12, Bars 12, Shockwave 12, VU Meter 1, Butterfly 1, Galaxy 1
motion share (DURATION_CELLABLE set): 24.3%      punctuation (On+Twinkle+Shockwave): 73.8%
median duration: 1,000 ms

Caveats — this fixture understates the real pipeline (24s song; a TestModel weave with a single Twinkle texture; no LLM-designed recipes; the second section is a peak whose fill/flash layers are punctuation-heavy by design) — but it demonstrates three things: the measurement is trivially reproducible in-repo; the median duration collapsed from 49–88s washes to 1.0s (the duration fix landed); and the share inversion is still visibly present in what the deterministic layers emit on their own (On is still the #1 effect). The real re-measurement (§3.1) must use a full generated show, but the direction is clear.

3. Proposed design

3.1 Re-measurement methodology — scripts/measure_fabric.py (proposed)

One script, two input modes, one report shape, so community files and our shows produce directly comparable tables:

# scripts/measure_fabric.py  (proposed)
def stats_from_instructions(instrs: list[dict], duration_s: float) -> FabricStats: ...
def stats_from_xsq(path: Path) -> FabricStats:
    """Parse <Effect> elements from an .xsq (community shows AND our finalized output)."""

@dataclass
class FabricStats:
    effects_per_min: float
    share_by_family: dict[str, float]        # motion / punctuation / bed / feature / other
    share_by_type: dict[str, float]          # top-N effect types
    duration_p50_by_type: dict[str, float]   # ms
    blend_mode_share: float                  # rows with T_CHOICE_LayerMethod set
    transition_share: float                  # rows with T_CHOICE_In/Out_Transition_Type
    value_curve_kinds: dict[str, float]      # brightness vs motion params
    layer_depth_hist: dict[int, int]
    per_prop_expansion: float | None         # see normalization below
    per_section: list[SectionStats]          # effects/min + motion share + treatment (Phase 2)
  • Inputs on our side: (a) the instructions cache of any real run (<cache_root>/<song_key>/instructions — a JSON list of EffectInstruction dumps, persisted at run.py:432–433 and refreshed post-refine at run.py:474–475), and (b) the golden fixture as the hermetic canary. Mode (b) makes the measurement runnable in CI forever; mode (a) is the honest number.
  • Inputs on the community side: the original 17 .xsq live in the user's xLights show folder, not the repo. The script's .xsq mode re-derives their stats on demand; additionally, freeze the community aggregates (the table in §2.1) into the script as constants so the comparison still runs without the corpus (see open question 1).
  • Normalization (the critical fix to the original comparison): community sequences target individual prop rows; ours target ~15 SEM_ groups, where one group-level effect animates every member prop. Define per_prop_expansion = mean member-count of the groups we actually target (derivable from rgbeffects.xml modelGroups — parsing already exists in xlights_core/knowledge/layout_semantics.py). Report both raw effects/min and prop-row-equivalent effects/min (raw × expansion for group-targeted rows). The tuning.py comment made this argument qualitatively; the script makes it a number.
  • Per-section, energy-bucketed: every stat is also reported per section with the section's intensity (and, post-Phase 2, its treatment), because a single whole-show number hides exactly the contrast improve-musicality is trying to create.

3.2 Targets — what “closed” means

Do not chase 58%/1,300 blindly. The community numbers come from different layouts (mega-displays sequenced per-prop), different tools (copy-paste reuse inflates counts), and a different authoring economy. Proposed target definition, energetic sections only (intensity ≥ 0.5, and post-Phase 2 treatments full/feature):

  1. Motion share ≥ 0.45 of instructions in energetic sections (today's advisory floor is 0.30; the fixture measures 0.24). Quiet/rest/gesture sections are exempt — deliberate stillness is a feature, not a regression.
  2. On + Twinkle combined ≤ 0.30 in energetic sections (they are ~62% of the golden fixture today). This targets the inversion directly rather than the total.
  3. Prop-row-equivalent density within ~2× of community typical in peak sections — i.e. raw group-row density ≈ 600–900/min at peak once expansion is accounted for, vs cell_budget's current 600/min ceiling.
  4. Pixel-level motion corroborates: I8's Tier 0 frame-delta metric (per-group motion from the .fseq) must rise alongside instruction-level motion share. Instruction counts are a proxy; photons are the product. If instruction share rises but rendered motion doesn't (e.g. cells too dim, occluded, or per-model-confined), the tuning is wrong even though the proxy improved.

3.3 Tuning sequence (one lever at a time, golden-regen per step)

  1. Rebalance before adding: reduce punctuation first — lower SPARKLE_TOP_N (12 → ~8) and let carrier_covers suppress more of the On accent layer (audit why 40 On rows survive in a fixture whose both sections have carriers — some are beds/washes, some are accents; the measurement's per-type-per-source breakdown answers this by tagging which realization layer emitted each instruction, see §3.5).
  2. Raise the weave's voice count: MAX_WOVEN_RECIPES 3 → 4 and add a second texture to fallback_weave when the section's vocabulary offers one.
  3. Raise the budget: BUDGET_SCALE 480 → ~700 (peak ≈ 820 cells/min) only after 1–2, and only if the I8 motion metric and a live watch agree the added cells read as fabric, not noise.
  4. Raise the advisory floor: MOTION_SHARE_MIN 0.30 → 0.40–0.45 (and consider scaling with intensity) so the Judge defends the new fabric.

3.4 Interaction with improve-musicality Phase 2 (treatments)

Phase 2 introduces treatment ∈ {full, feature, pulse, gesture, rest} and makes realize_section withhold layers — deliberate sparseness. Consequences for this item:

  • Per-treatment density targets, not one number. The measurement report groups by treatment; the gap is evaluated only on full/feature sections. A show-level effects/min that drops after Phase 2 is expected and correct.
  • Ordering: run the baseline measurement before Phase 2 lands (so the current gap is quantified against current semantics), but do the tuning after or alongside Phase 2 — otherwise dials get tuned for a realization model that's about to change. The roadmap's sequencing table (I7 in the “continuous hardening” slot, after F-A phases) agrees.
  • Shared metric plumbing: Phase 2's advisory dynamic-range/focus-budget metrics (planned qa/musicality.py) and this item's motion/density stats want the same per-section instruction aggregation — build FabricStats.per_section so qa/musicality.py can import it rather than re-deriving.

3.5 Attribution: which layer emits what

Tuning blind-spots come from not knowing whether an On row is a bed, an accent, or an LLM wash. EffectInstruction carries no provenance. Proposed: a transient source tag (e.g. "weave" | "accents" | "bed" | "triggers" | "flash" | "generator" | "vu" | "composite") — either an excluded-from-dump field on EffectInstruction or a parallel counter returned by realize_section — surfaced only in the measurement report. This keeps the cache/golden formats unchanged (mark as a design decision: if it must persist, the golden fixture regenerates once).

4. Implementation plan

  1. scripts/measure_fabric.py with stats_from_instructions + stats_from_xsq + frozen community aggregates; unit tests for both parsers (a tiny synthetic .xsq fixture; the golden instructions file).
  2. Baseline report: run it on (a) the golden fixture, (b) at least one full real run's instructions cache (Candy Cane Lane / Mad Russian Christmas re-run on current code), and — if the show folder is at hand — (c) 2–3 community .xsq to validate the parser against the 2026-06-11 numbers. Commit the report as docs/effects-layering-analysis-2026-07.md (the successor snapshot).
  3. Per-group expansion factor: compute from rgbeffects modelGroups; add the prop-row-equivalent column.
  4. Decide targets (per §3.2) in that doc; get the numbers into tuning.py comments so the dials cite their evidence, matching house style (“Revision-log analysis (42 runs): …”).
  5. Tune levers in §3.3 order, one per commit, each with: golden regen (XLO_REGEN_GOLDEN=1), a re-measurement delta in the commit message, and (for budget raises) an I8-Tier-0 or live spot-check.
  6. Guard the result: a hermetic tests/test_fabric_stats.py asserting loose bounds on the golden fixture (e.g. motion share ≥ 0.35, On+Twinkle ≤ 0.45 in the energetic section) so a future change can't silently re-invert the fabric.
  7. Advisory floor update (MOTION_SHARE_MIN) once the generated shows clear it — raising the floor before the generator clears it just spams the Judge.

5. Testing strategy

  • Hermetic measurement tests: stats_from_instructions against hand-built instruction lists (known shares/durations); stats_from_xsq against a ≤20-effect synthetic .xsq written by the test (ElementTree — no xLights needed); golden-fixture stats as a canary with loose bounds.
  • Golden discipline: every deliberate dial change regenerates the golden snapshot exactly once, in its own commit, so git log fixtures/golden_instructions.json reads as the tuning history. Non-tuning commits must leave it byte-identical.
  • QA-threshold tests: extend the existing motion-share advisory tests in tests/ (rules QA) when MOTION_SHARE_MIN moves — assert the finding fires below and stays silent above the new floor, at the intensity gate.
  • Live verification (non-CI): one attended run per major dial change, watched against the review bundles (visual_review/iterN), because “denser” can read as “noisier” — the failure mode no instruction-count test can see. I8's motion heatmap makes this cheaper.

7. Acceptance criteria

  • [ ] scripts/measure_fabric.py produces the §3.1 report from both an instructions cache and an .xsq, with unit tests for both paths.
  • [ ] A committed 2026-07 re-measurement doc quantifies the current gap on ≥1 full real show, with the prop-row-equivalent normalization and per-section (intensity-bucketed) breakdown.
  • [ ] Explicit targets (motion share, On+Twinkle share, density band) are recorded with rationale, replacing “community does 58%” as the goalpost.
  • [ ] At least the rebalance levers (accents down, recipes/fallback up) are tuned against the measurement, each with a golden regen and a before/after delta.
  • [ ] MOTION_SHARE_MIN reflects the new floor and its comment cites the re-measurement.
  • [ ] A hermetic fabric-stats canary test guards against re-inversion.
  • [ ] Density targets are treatment-aware: the measurement and the canary exempt quiet/rest/gesture sections, so improve-musicality Phase 2's deliberate sparseness cannot register as a density regression.

8. Open questions

  1. Community corpus custody: should a redacted per-file stats JSON (not the .xsq themselves — they're licensed vendor content) be checked into docs/ so the comparison is fully reproducible in-repo?
  2. Measure the .fseq instead? Instruction counts are the author-side proxy; I8's Tier 0 measures the rendered result. Should the canary bounds eventually move to pixel metrics entirely and let instruction stats be diagnostic only? (Leaning yes, post-I8.)
  3. Layer depth: community stacks to 19 layers; our hard cap is 4 (clamp_layer_budget, catalog rule #10). Is raising the cap on the hero/focal group (only) in scope here, or a separate change? The composite stacks already push against it.
  4. Where do the frozen targets live — tuning.py comments, the analysis doc, or a small fabric_targets.py the canary test imports? (Recommend: doc for prose + the canary's literals as the single enforced copy.)
  5. Density vs song genre: the two generated shows are both high-energy Christmas pop. Should targets be conditioned on the brief's overall energy (a ballad show legitimately measuring “sparse”)? Per-section intensity bucketing may already cover this.
